Genetic Selection for Accelerated Learning and Olfactory Detection of Explosives

The Williams Lab develops and validates a series of behavioral strategies to reduce the time frame for training large groups of rats to reliably search for, identify and distinguish TNT explosive residues, chemical pathogens and other odorants of interest from a number of distractor odors. Our initial studies evaluated a number of operant approaches to accelerate explosive detection responding in Sprague Dawley lab rats. Our current studies evaluate the effectiveness of this six-phase training regimen in producing high levels of accurate explosive detection and extended marking behavior in African Gambian pouched rats. The overall goals of this project are to provide public service agencies with proven approaches to detect hidden explosives through the use of bio-detecting rodents as well as more advanced rodents with genetically enhanced olfactory identification systems.

(Funded by research support from DARPA to Ophir, A. Cornell Univ.; Williams, C. L., Univ. of Virginia; and Feinstein, P., Hunter College).
